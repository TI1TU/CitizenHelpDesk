In rain, smooth and calm driving is mandatory, not optional.

  • Deep trenches / construction pits

Motorcycles and scooters are extremely risky in heavy rain.

  • Avoid sudden leaning while turning
  • Use rear brake lightly, rely more on front + engine braking
  • Keep headlights on to stay visible
  • Wear reflective raincoat / rain gear
  • Avoid riding through deep water whenever possible

Motorbike skidding is one of the most common monsoon accident types.

Overtaking in rain is much more dangerous than in dry weather.

  • Tyres losing grip while changing lanes
  • Oncoming traffic hidden by spray
  • Longer braking distance if someone brakes suddenly

Only overtake if absolutely necessary and visibility is clear.

  2. Exit safely if water level is rising
  3. Move yourself and passengers to higher / safer ground
  4. Call towing, RSA (Roadside Assistance) or your insurance helpline

‚úÖ 13. Tips for Monsoon Driving Safety

  • Avoid overtaking unless really necessary
  • Keep windows slightly open or use AC correctly if fog builds up
  • Replace old or hard wiper blades
  • Check tyre pressure and tread depth weekly
