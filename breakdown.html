A vehicle is considered ‚Äúbroken down‚Äù when it cannot move safely on its own due to a fault or damage.

  • Engine stops suddenly

  • Vehicle overheats
  • Brake failure
  • Battery dies
  • Fuel finishes
  • Cable / wiring fault
  • Accident damage makes it undrivable

A stopped vehicle on the road is a serious hazard if not handled properly.

Stay calm. Most breakdown injuries happen because people panic and:

  • Run suddenly into traffic
  • Stand in the middle of the road
  • Open doors without checking behind

Take a deep breath and follow the steps below calmly.

If possible:

  • Move vehicle to extreme left side of the road

On highways, move to the service lane if it is safe to do so.

Mandatory:

  • Turn ON hazard lights as soon as you stop
  • Keep them ON until help arrives or vehicle moves
  • Use parking lights at night if required

This warns other drivers that your vehicle is not moving normally.

According to safety guidelines:

  • Place the red emergency triangle about 50 metres behind your vehicle on city roads
  • Place it about 100 metres behind on highways
  • At night: use reflector + hazard lights

If you don‚Äôt have a triangle, you can temporarily use:

  • Bright cloth or jacket

  • Stone with cloth tied around it
  • Blinking indicator torch

But a proper emergency triangle is always best.

People standing near broken vehicles are often hit by fast-moving vehicles.

  • Stand on footpath or off-road area
  • Move passengers to a safe place away from traffic
  • Move kids and elders away from vehicle
  • Do not sit inside the broken car on highway shoulder for long

Most deaths in breakdowns happen because people stand too close to the live traffic lane.

You can call:

  • 112 ‚Äì National emergency number
  • Your insurance helpline (many offer free towing)
  • Highway patrol / state toll helpline
  • RSA (Roadside Assistance) service providers
  • Local mechanic or known garage

Share your exact location using Google Maps or nearby landmarks so help can find you quickly.

If your bike or scooter breaks down:

  • Move it to extreme left, out of the traffic lane
  • Do NOT repair it in the middle of the lane
  • Switch on indicator / hazard flasher if available
  • Park away from sharp turns
  • Avoid pushing the bike in the middle of busy road
  • At night, wear bright/reflective clothing if possible

Bikes are almost invisible at night ‚Äì extra caution is needed.

üöô 9. Breakdown Rules for Cars

  • Bring the car slowly to the left side
  • Turn the steering to the left after stopping
  • Switch on hazard lights immediately
  • Place warning triangle at correct distance
  • If you see smoke from bonnet, move away ‚Äì do not open it immediately

Cars stopped without warning are often hit from behind, especially at night.

üöõ 10. Breakdown Rules for Trucks & Heavy Vehicles

Truck drivers must:

  • Place two triangles ‚Äì one in front and one behind
  • Ensure reflector strips on rear/side are visible
  • Use wheel chocks to prevent the vehicle from rolling
  • Switch on parking lights at night
  • Inform transport company or control room if vehicle blocks a lane

Truck breakdowns often block full lanes and attract heavy penalties if not properly marked.

üö´ 12. What NOT to Do During Breakdown

  • Do not stand directly behind or in front of your vehicle
  • Do not repair in the middle of the traffic lane
  • Do not push car on highway lanes unless absolutely unavoidable
  • Do not rely only on phone flashlight as warning
  • Do not stop on blind turns or slopes if you can avoid it
  • Do not wave hands dangerously close to moving traffic

üõ†Ô∏è 13. Common Causes of Breakdown

  • Old or weak battery
  • Low engine oil or coolant
  • Overheating due to poor maintenance
  • Running on very low fuel regularly
  • Worn-out tyres
  • Damaged alternator or wiring
  • Poor or irregular vehicle servicing

Regular servicing and basic checks can prevent many breakdowns.

üìã 14. Quick Summary ‚Äì Breakdown Safety Rules

  • Move vehicle safely to the left side
  • Hazard lights ON immediately
  • Place triangle: 50m (city), 100m (highway)

  • Call emergency / roadside help
  • Fines apply for unsafe stopping and obstruction
