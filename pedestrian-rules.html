Pedestrian & Zebra Crossing Rules

Driver & pedestrian responsibilities explained for safe crossing.

1. What Is a Zebra Crossing?

A zebra crossing is a marked pedestrian path with white road stripes. Its purpose is to give pedestrians a safe place to cross. Drivers must slow down and allow people to cross.

2. When Drivers MUST Stop at Zebra Crossing

  • A pedestrian is already crossing
  • Someone is about to step onto the crossing
  • Pedestrian signal is green
  • Traffic signal is red
  • Elderly, kids, pregnant women or disabled are crossing
  • Police or traffic volunteer signals to stop
  • Vehicle ahead has stopped for pedestrians

3. When Drivers Should NOT Stop on Zebra Crossing

  • Stopping ON the white stripes
  • Blocking the crossing during red signal
  • Overtaking another vehicle at a crossing
  • Moving forward when pedestrians have right of way

4. Rights & Duties of Pedestrians

  • Use only marked zebra crossings
  • Obey pedestrian signals
  • Look left–right–left before stepping
  • Avoid running suddenly onto the road

Safety is both ways — pedestrians must also follow rules.

5. Common Illegal Behaviours by Drivers

  • Not stopping for pedestrians
  • Speeding near crossings
  • Honking to hurry pedestrians
  • Parking or stopping on zebra crossing
  • Overtaking near crossings

6. Common Mistakes by Pedestrians

  • Crossing anywhere instead of zebra crossing
  • Using mobile while crossing
  • Running between vehicles
  • Ignoring pedestrian signals
  • Avoiding flyover/FOB

7. Fines Related to Zebra Crossings

For drivers:

  • ₹500–₹1,000 for not giving way
  • ₹500–₹1,000 for stopping on zebra crossing
  • ₹1,000–₹2,000 for dangerous driving near crossings

For pedestrians:

  • ₹100–₹500 for jaywalking (in select cities)

8. Special Rules in School Zones

  • Slow down to minimum speed
  • Watch for children stepping suddenly
  • No unnecessary honking
  • Stop completely when guard signals

9. Myths vs Reality

Myth: Vehicles have priority.
Reality: Pedestrians have right of way at zebra crossings.

Myth: I can cross anywhere.
Reality: Jaywalking is unsafe & punishable.

10. Quick Summary

  • Drivers must stop at zebra crossings
  • Never block the crossing
  • Pedestrians must use marked crossings
  • Fines ₹500–₹1,000 for violations
  • Extra caution in school zones
