Driving Essentials

Documents You Must Carry While Driving in India

Driving without proper documents can lead to fines, trouble with police, or even your vehicle being detained. Here’s a simple explanation for every driver and rider in India.

1. Why These Documents Matter

Traffic rules come from the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Police can ask you to show your documents anytime during checking. If you cannot show them (physical or digital), you may receive a challan.

2. Mandatory Documents You Must Carry

1. Driving Licence (DL)

Your DL proves that you are legally allowed to drive.

  • No licence at all → serious offence.
  • Have licence but forgot it → smaller offence but still a fine.
  • You can show DL in DigiLocker or mParivahan.

Tip: Keep your DL in your wallet + also save it digitally.

2. Registration Certificate (RC)

  • No RC → vehicle may be detained.
  • RC exists but not carried → fine for “not producing documents”.
  • Digital RC is accepted in many states.

Tip: Keep a photo + physical card + DigiLocker version.

3. Insurance (Third-Party Minimum)

  • Valid insurance is compulsory.
  • Expired insurance → heavy fine.
  • Repeat offences may require court appearance.
  • Insurance PDF on phone is usually accepted.

Tip: Set a reminder before expiry and renew on time.

4. Pollution Under Control (PUC)

  • No PUC / expired PUC → fine.
  • Metro cities check PUC more strictly.
  • PUC renewal is cheap and quick.

Tip: Keep a photo of your PUC slip on your phone.

3. Are Digital Documents Accepted?

Yes, MOST states accept:

  • DigiLocker
  • mParivahan

These apps show official data, but some officers may still prefer physical copies.

Best practice: Carry both physical and digital copies.

4. What Happens if You Can’t Show Documents?

Police may:

  • Issue a challan
  • Impound your vehicle (in serious cases)
  • Ask you to appear in court
  • Add extra penalty for non-cooperation

Tip: Stay calm, be polite, and show documents clearly.

5. Quick Summary

Carry these documents every time:

  • Driving Licence (DL)
  • Registration Certificate (RC)
  • Insurance
  • PUC Certificate

Best: Keep all in DigiLocker + also carry physical copies.
