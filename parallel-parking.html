Parallel parking is the most common type of roadside parking in India. This guide explains how to parallel park, where it is allowed, mistakes to avoid and fines.

Parallel parking means parking your vehicle in line with the road, between two vehicles or within marked boxes.

In simple words:

  • Your car is parallel to the road
  • Parked in a straight line
  • In a slot between two vehicles or markings

Common locations:

  • Market areas
  • Residential streets
  • Office areas
  • Public roadside parking zones

‚úÖ 2. Where Parallel Parking Is Allowed

You can usually parallel park:

  • Where parking is permitted by signs
  • Inside marked white parking boxes
  • In designated side parking lanes
  • In authorised residential parking areas
  • In public roadside parking zones

Only park where boards / markings clearly allow parking.

üö´ 3. Where Parallel Parking Is NOT Allowed

Do not parallel park:

  • Near fire hydrants
  • At bus stops or auto stands
  • On corners or sharp curves
  • On pedestrian crossings (zebra crossings)
  • On footpaths
  • In front of school or hospital gates
  • In clearly marked No-Parking zones
  • In front of private gates / entrances
  • On narrow lanes where traffic will get blocked
  • Near traffic signals and junctions

Wrong parking in these places is punishable and vehicles may be towed.

  1. Find a space that is slightly bigger than your car.
  2. Turn on left indicator to inform vehicles behind.
  3. Align your car ‚Äì bring your rear wheel near the rear bumper of the car in front.
  4. Reverse slowly and turn steering fully towards the kerb.
  5. Slide into the slot at a slow, controlled angle.
  6. Straighten steering once the car is halfway in.
  7. Adjust forward/back to centre your car between vehicles.
  8. Leave 1‚Äì2 feet space in front and at the back.

üìè 5. Distance Rules You Must Follow

  • Keep about 30 cm (1 foot) distance from the kerb
  • Do not block the moving traffic lane
  • Do not extend your car into the driving lane
  • Ensure your wheels are straight and not rubbing the kerb

Parking too far from the kerb or at a sharp angle is dangerous and may be considered improper parking.

üåô 6. Special Rules for Night Parking

At night:

  • Switch off headlights once parked
  • Turn on parking lights if the area is dark
  • Avoid very dark, isolated spots where your car is not visible
  • Do not park near blind turns or slopes

Many night-time side-impact crashes happen because parked vehicles are not clearly visible.
