Blind Spots in Vehicles

What blind spots are, how accidents happen, and how drivers can avoid them.

1. What Is a Blind Spot?

A blind spot is an area around your vehicle that you cannot see even with:

  • Rear-view mirror
  • Left side mirror
  • Right side mirror

These areas can hide bikes, cycles, pedestrians and small vehicles.

2. Blind Spots in a Car

Cars have 4 major blind spots:

  • Left rear side
  • Right rear side
  • A-pillars near windshield (blocks view while turning)
  • Directly behind the car

These areas are responsible for many lane-change accidents.

3. Blind Spots in a Bike (Two-Wheeler)

  • Area directly behind the rider
  • Small zones on left & right side
  • Blind zone while turning head
  • When overtaking larger vehicles

Bike riders must double-check mirrors before any movement.

4. Blind Spots in Trucks & Heavy Vehicles

These are the most dangerous blind spots:

  • Directly in front of the truck
  • Left and right sides (very large area)
  • Directly behind the truck
  • Near rear wheels

If you cannot see the truck driver’s mirror, they cannot see you.

5. How to Avoid Staying in Blind Spots

  • Maintain safe distance
  • Avoid riding next to trucks for long
  • Never cut in front of a large vehicle
  • Do not stop very close behind buses/trucks
  • Use indicators early
  • Adjust mirrors correctly
  • Turn your head slightly to check blind zones

6. How to Adjust Mirrors Properly

Car mirrors:

  • Side mirrors should show less of your own car body
  • Mirror coverage should connect with rear-view mirror
  • No large gap between mirror views

Bike mirrors:

  • Half road + half lane behind should be visible
  • Minimum handle/shoulder visibility

7. Common Blind Spot Accident Situations

  • Overtaking trucks from the left side
  • Riding next to buses for long
  • Sudden lane changes without mirror check
  • Bike riding too close behind cars
  • Car reversing without checking surroundings
  • Truck turning left suddenly (very common)
  • Bikes squeezing between lanes

8. Tips for Car Drivers

  • Do a quick shoulder-check before lane change
  • Check mirrors every 5–8 seconds
  • Avoid sudden lane changes
  • Never overtake trucks/buses from the left
  • Use indicators early
  • Do not rely on mirrors alone

9. Tips for Bike Riders

  • Never stay beside trucks or buses
  • Do not ride in a large vehicle’s blind zone
  • Ride slightly ahead or behind, not beside
  • Avoid squeezing between lanes
  • Reduce speed near large vehicles

10. Quick Summary

  • All vehicles have blind spots
  • Trucks have the biggest danger zones
  • Avoid left side of heavy vehicles
  • Check mirrors + shoulder before lane change
  • Stay visible and maintain distance
