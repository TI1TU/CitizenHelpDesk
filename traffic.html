Traffic Rules & Challans

Understand helmet rules, challans, documents and your basic rights on the road.

ü™ñ Helmet & Seat Belt Rules

Helmet: Mandatory for rider and pillion. Only ISI-marked helmets are allowed.

  • Rider + pillion must wear helmet with strap locked.
  • Fines apply if anyone on bike is without helmet.
  • Children on two-wheelers: follow latest safety rules in your state.

Seat Belt: Front and rear seat belts are compulsory in cars.

üìÉ Documents Required While Driving

Always keep valid copies of:

  • Driving Licence (DL)
  • Registration Certificate (RC)
  • Insurance Certificate
  • Pollution Certificate (PUC)

You can store these in DigiLocker or mParivahan. In most places digital copies are accepted.

üí∏ Challans & Fines

Common reasons for challan:

  • No helmet / no seat belt
  • Signal jump or wrong-side driving
  • Overspeeding
  • Driving without valid documents

How to check challan: Use your state traffic police website, e-Challan portal or mParivahan app with your vehicle number.

üåê How to Pay Challan Online

  1. Open your state traffic police or echallan.parivahan.gov.in.
  2. Enter vehicle number / challan number.
  3. Verify details and select pending challan.
  4. Pay using UPI, net banking, card, etc.
  5. Download and save the payment receipt.

Always use official websites (.gov.in / .nic.in). Avoid agents or random payment links.

üöó When Police Stop You

  • Stay calm and park safely on the side.
  • Police can ask for DL, RC, PUC and Insurance.
  • Digital documents from DigiLocker / mParivahan are usually valid.
  • They should not forcibly take away your vehicle keys without reason.

If you feel harassed, note the officer‚Äôs name and ID and contact the local traffic helpline or file a complaint through official channels.

‚ùì Quick FAQs

Q. Is triple riding allowed?
No. Only 2 persons are allowed on most two-wheelers.

Q. Is mobile use allowed while driving?
Using mobile while driving is an offence. Use hands-free only where allowed by law.

Q. Are number plate designs allowed?
No fancy fonts, stickers or designs. Use standard HSRP plates.
