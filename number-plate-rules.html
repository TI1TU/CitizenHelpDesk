Number Plate Rules in India (2025)

Legal format, color, font, HSRP rules & fines—explained simply for all vehicle owners.

1. Why Number Plate Rules Matter

The number plate shows your vehicle's official identity.

  • Helps police detect violations
  • Required for insurance & challan systems
  • Prevents misuse & crimes
  • Must follow AIS-159 standards

2. Legal Number Plate Format (2025)

A correct plate must follow this format:

  • State Code – e.g., MH, DL, UP, KA
  • RTO Code – e.g., 12, 03
  • Series – e.g., AB
  • Number – e.g., 1234

Example: MH 12 AB 1234

3. Legal Appearance (Required)

  • White plate + black letters → private vehicles
  • Yellow plate + black letters → commercial
  • Green plate → electric vehicles
  • Black plate + yellow letters → rental/self-drive
  • Red plate with white letters → temporary registration
  • IND HSRP recommended (hologram + chakra)
  • All letters same size, clean & readable

4. Illegal Number Plate Styles

These will lead to challan:

  • Fancy fonts
  • LED or glowing plates
  • Hindi/Marathi/Gujarati fonts
  • Names or stickers on plate
  • Diagonal or slanted writing
  • Broken or damaged plates
  • Plates covered by glass/plastic
  • Inside windshield plates

5. What Is HSRP?

HSRP (High Security Registration Plate) includes:

  • IND hologram
  • Laser-etched number
  • Chrome chakra
  • Snap-lock rivets

Mandatory in many states. Apply online on your state's HSRP portal.

6. Fines for Wrong Number Plates

  • ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 → wrong format
  • ₹500 – ₹1,000 → damaged plates
  • ₹3,000 – ₹5,000 → fancy/LED plates

E-challan may be issued automatically if camera cannot read the plate.

7. Why Fancy Plates Are Dangerous

  • Camera misreads numbers
  • Police misidentification
  • Hard to track stolen vehicles
  • Challan errors

Simple plates are safest.

8. Do’s and Don’ts

DO:

  • Use HSRP
  • Keep plate clean
  • Replace damaged plates
  • Use correct font & colors

DON'T:

  • Use artwork or symbols
  • Use plastic covers
  • Use tiny plates
  • Hide any number

Quick Summary

  • Must follow AIS-159 rules
  • No fancy or LED plates
  • White = private, Yellow = commercial
  • HSRP recommended
  • Fines ₹1,000–₹5,000
