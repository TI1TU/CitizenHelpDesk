Ambulance & Emergency Vehicle Right-of-Way Rules

Giving way is not a formality — it saves lives.

1. What Is an Emergency Vehicle?

Emergency vehicles include:

  • Ambulances
  • Fire brigade
  • Police response vehicles
  • Disaster management vehicles
  • Emergency medical vans

They have legal right-of-way.

2. What Drivers MUST Do When Ambulance Approaches

  • Move to the left slowly
  • Create space for ambulance to pass
  • Stop if required
  • Stay calm — no sudden braking
  • Follow hand signals of emergency driver
  • Let ambulance pass fully before moving
  • Use indicators if slowing down
  • Do NOT follow behind the ambulance

3. What Drivers MUST NOT Do

  • Block the ambulance
  • Race with ambulance
  • Follow (tail) the ambulance
  • Cut in front of ambulance
  • Suddenly brake in its path
  • Ignore siren or lights

Blocking an ambulance is a serious offence.

4. What to Do in Heavy Traffic

  • Move slowly to the left
  • Create a narrow lane (“ambulance corridor”)
  • Avoid honking
  • Do not jump lanes suddenly
  • Let the ambulance pass even if movement is small

5. What to Do at Traffic Signals

If your signal is red:

  • Stay where you are
  • Do NOT cross red signal illegally
  • Let traffic in front adjust
  • Follow police hand signals

If your signal is green:

  • Clear the junction safely
  • Do not panic or accelerate harshly

6. Highway Rules for Ambulance

  • Move to leftmost lane
  • Reduce speed gradually
  • Do NOT brake instantly
  • Do NOT overtake ambulance
  • Keep safe distance always

7. Fines for Blocking Ambulance

  • ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 for not giving way
  • ₹5,000 – ₹10,000 for deliberate blocking
  • Court action in serious cases
  • CCTV-based challans common in metros

Some states treat this as dangerous driving.

8. Why Giving Way Matters

Giving way helps emergency teams reach faster and saves lives.

  • Patients reach hospital quicker
  • Fire brigade reaches emergencies on time
  • Police respond faster

9. Myths vs Reality

Myth: Following ambulance helps avoid traffic.
Reality: Illegal and dangerous.

Myth: One car blocking won't matter.
Reality: It can delay life-saving treatment.

Myth: Ambulance will find another route.
Reality: In traffic, your lane may be the only option.

10. Quick Summary

  • Move left & give way
  • Never block or race
  • No following ambulance
  • Fines up to ₹10,000
  • Giving way saves lives
