Road Marking Rules in India

Yellow lines, white lines, arrows & lane symbols explained in simple language.

1. Why Road Markings Matter

Road markings are silent instructions painted on the road. They help:

  • Maintain lane discipline
  • Reduce accidents
  • Indicate overtaking zones
  • Guide turns & roundabouts
  • Warn about hazards

Ignoring markings is a traffic violation.

2. Main Types of Road Lines

Common line types on Indian roads:

  • Single white line
  • Double white line
  • Single yellow line
  • Double yellow line
  • Broken (dashed) lines

3. Single White Line

Meaning:

  • Stay in your lane
  • Lane change allowed with caution
  • Overtaking allowed only if safe

Used on city roads and divided roads. Do not cut sharply across it.

4. Double White Line

Meaning:

  • No lane change
  • No overtaking
  • Stay strictly in your lane

Used near signals, curves, steep roads and accident-prone areas.

5. Single Yellow Line

Meaning:

  • Indicates separation of opposite traffic
  • Stay on your side
  • Overtaking must be very cautious

Common on two-lane roads without physical dividers.

6. Double Yellow Line

Meaning:

  • Strictly no overtaking
  • Do not cross the line
  • Acts like a solid divider

Crossing double yellow lines is extremely dangerous and a serious violation.

7. Broken (Dashed) Lines

Meaning:

  • Overtaking allowed if safe
  • Lane change allowed

Used on highways and open stretches. Always check mirrors and blind spots first.

8. Arrows on the Road (Lane Direction)

  • Straight arrow: Go straight only.
  • Left-only arrow: Only left turn allowed.
  • Right-only arrow: Right turn/U-turn only.
  • Straight + Right: Straight or right allowed.
  • Straight + Left: Straight or left allowed.

Select your lane before the junction, not at the last second.

9. Zig-Zag Yellow Markings

Meaning:

  • School or pedestrian zone
  • High caution
  • Slow down
  • No overtaking

10. Stop Line

A thick white line before signals, zebra crossings or check posts.

  • Stop before the line
  • Do not stop on or after it

Stopping on the line can attract camera-based challans.

11. Box Junction (Yellow Grid)

Meaning: Do not enter unless your exit is clear.

Purpose: Prevent blocking intersections and gridlock.

12. Speed Breaker Markings

White triangles or patterns before bumps indicate:

  • Speed breaker ahead
  • Slow down
  • Prepare to stop

13. Myths vs Reality

Myth: Only signals matter, not lines.
Reality: Lines are legally enforceable.

Myth: I can cross any line if road is empty.
Reality: Still illegal and risky.

Myth: Broken line means drive fast.
Reality: You still must ensure safety.

14. Quick Summary

  • White lines = lane discipline
  • Yellow lines = opposite traffic separation
  • Double lines = no overtaking or crossing
  • Arrows = follow lane direction
  • Stop before stop line, not on it
