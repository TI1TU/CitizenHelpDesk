No Parking Rules in India

Where parking is banned, where it's allowed, towing rules, and fines explained.

1. What Is a No-Parking Zone?

A no-parking zone is an area where stopping, standing, waiting, or leaving your vehicle is NOT allowed.

Common signs include:

  • “NO PARKING” boards
  • “TOW AWAY ZONE” boards
  • Red crossed P symbol
  • Yellow/black painted kerbs

If any of these signs appear → parking is illegal.

2. Where Parking Is ILLEGAL (Strictly Prohibited)

You must NEVER park at:

  • Bus stops
  • Taxi and auto stands
  • Fire hydrants
  • Hospital gates
  • School/college gates
  • Metro or railway entrances
  • Road corners or blind turns
  • Footpaths and pedestrian walkways
  • Zebra crossings
  • Near traffic signals
  • Flyover entry/exit
  • On bridges
  • Middle of the road
  • In front of private/public gates
  • Narrow streets blocking traffic
  • Areas marked “Tow Away”

Parking in these areas may lead to towing.

3. Where Parking Is ALLOWED (Legal)

  • Designated parking zones
  • Paid parking lots
  • Residential parking areas
  • White-marked boxes on the road
  • Mall/market parking areas
  • Open areas without parking restrictions
  • Street-side parking only if there is no restriction sign

4. Rules for Parking Near Junctions

  • Do NOT park within 50 meters of traffic signals
  • Do NOT park within 20 meters of intersections

Parking here blocks visibility and causes accidents → illegal.

5. Night Parking Rules

  • Turn wheels toward kerb if on slope
  • Avoid dark isolated areas
  • Make sure the vehicle doesn’t obstruct the road
  • Use parking lights for large vehicles

Cars left in dark areas are more likely to be hit.

6. Towing Rules – When Police Can Tow

Your vehicle can be towed if parked:

  • In a no-parking zone
  • On footpaths
  • Blocking traffic
  • In school or hospital zones
  • In “Tow Away Zone” areas
  • Near VIP routes

After towing, it is taken to the nearest impound yard.

7. What Happens After Towing?

  • Visit police yard
  • Show DL + RC
  • Pay fine + towing charges
  • Vehicle is released

Charges differ from city to city.

8. Fines for No Parking

  • ₹500 – ₹1,000 for general no-parking
  • ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 for obstruction of traffic
  • Towing charges additional
  • CCTV challans common in metro cities
  • Harsh penalties for blocking ambulances/school gates

9. Tips to Avoid Parking Fines

  • Check for “No Parking” boards
  • Never park on footpaths
  • Never park on turns
  • Use Google Maps to find parking areas
  • Don’t double-park
  • Always park inside marked white boxes
  • Use paid parking — it’s cheaper than challan

10. Myths vs Reality

Myth: Hazard lights make parking legal.
Reality: Still illegal.

Myth: Parking for 1 minute is okay.
Reality: Illegal even for 10 seconds.

Myth: If no police nearby, I can park anywhere.
Reality: CCTV catches you.

Myth: Footpath parking is okay if road is narrow.
Reality: 100% illegal.

11. Quick Summary

  • No parking at signals, gates, footpaths, corners
  • Tow-away zones = zero stopping allowed
  • Fines: ₹500–₹2,000 + towing charges
  • Park only in marked or legal areas
  • Wrong parking blocks emergency vehicles
