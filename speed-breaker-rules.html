Speed Breaker & Road Bump Rules (India 2025)

Legal height, placement rules, types of bumps & safe driving tips.

1. What Is a Speed Breaker?

A raised structure built to slow down vehicles and protect pedestrians near sensitive zones.

2. Where Speed Breakers Are Allowed

  • Schools, hospitals, markets
  • Residential colonies
  • Accident-prone zones
  • Sharp curves
  • Pedestrian crossings, T-intersections

3. Where Speed Breakers Are NOT Allowed

  • Highways, expressways
  • Flyovers or bridges
  • Main arterial roads
  • Steep slopes
  • Roads without proper signboards

4. Legal Dimensions (Government Rules)

  • Height: 8–10 cm
  • Width: 3–3.7 meters
  • Shape: Rounded (not sharp)
  • Paint: Black & white stripes
  • Mandatory warning signboard

5. Types of Speed Breakers

  • Standard speed breaker
  • Speed hump
  • Rumble strips
  • Speed table

6. How to Drive Over Speed Breakers Safely

  • Slow down in advance
  • Cross gently at 5–10 km/h
  • Don’t zig-zag or overtake
  • Keep distance from heavy vehicles
  • Avoid accelerating before finishing the hump

7. Penalties Related to Speed Breakers

Drivers:

  • Overspeeding → challan
  • Overtaking near bump → challan
  • Sudden braking → dangerous driving

Authorities:

  • Illegal speed breakers → violation
  • No signage → unsafe construction

8. Signs Before Speed Breakers

  • Warning board
  • Reflective markings
  • Visible at night
  • Black/white stripes

9. Myths vs Reality

  • Myth: Higher bumps reduce accidents → False
  • Myth: Small bumps don't cause damage → False
  • Myth: Overtaking near bump is safe → Very unsafe

Quick Summary

  • Speed breakers only in legal zones
  • Height must be 8–10 cm
  • No bumps on highways
  • Overspeeding near bumps = dangerous
  • Illegal bumps can be reported
