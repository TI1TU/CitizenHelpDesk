U-Turn Rules in India (2025)

When U-turns are allowed · Illegal U-turns · Fines · Safe turning tips.

1. What Is a U-Turn?

A U-turn means turning your vehicle around to go in the opposite direction. It must be done only where rules allow it.

2. When U-Turn Is Allowed

  • There is a U-turn signboard
  • Road markings show U-turn
  • Traffic police allows it
  • Road is clear & safe
  • You use indicators properly
  • You follow lane discipline

3. When U-Turn Is NOT Allowed

  • No U-turn sign
  • Red signal arrow
  • Busy intersections
  • Zebra crossings
  • Flyovers & bridges
  • Narrow roads
  • Blind turns
  • Wrong-side turning
  • Blocking other vehicles

4. Fines for Illegal U-Turn

  • ₹500 – ₹1,000 → illegal U-turn
  • ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 → dangerous turning
  • E-challan through CCTV

5. How to Take a Safe U-Turn

  1. Move to right lane
  2. Slow down early
  3. Turn on right indicator
  4. Check mirrors & blind spot
  5. Turn slowly and smoothly
  6. Join the correct lane after turning

6. Dangerous U-Turn Mistakes

  • Sudden U-turn from middle lane
  • No indicator
  • Cutting across lanes
  • U-turn in heavy traffic
  • Blind U-turn on highways
  • Following others blindly

7. Highway & Expressway Rules

  • Only use official U-turn openings
  • Never cross median illegally
  • Slow down well before the opening
  • Maintain distance from trucks/buses

8. Myths vs Reality

  • Myth: “It’s a small turn.” → Dangerous
  • Myth: “Everyone turns here.” → Still illegal
  • Myth: “No sign = allowed.” → Not true
  • Myth: “Fast U-turn is safer.” → Very risky

Quick Summary

  • U-turn only where allowed
  • Illegal U-turn fine: ₹500–₹1,000
  • No U-turn near signals/flyovers
  • Use indicator & slow down
  • Stay in right lane for U-turn
