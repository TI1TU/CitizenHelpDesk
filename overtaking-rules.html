Overtaking Rules in India

When you can overtake, when it’s illegal, and how to stay safe on the road.

1. What Is Overtaking?

Overtaking means passing a slower vehicle ahead by using another lane.

Done correctly → safe
Done incorrectly → fatal accident

2. When Overtaking Is Allowed

  • Road ahead is clear
  • No oncoming vehicle
  • No blind curve or hill
  • Front vehicle is moving slow
  • Enough space to complete overtake
  • Proper indicators used
  • No pedestrian or cycle ahead
  • You return to your lane safely

3. When Overtaking Is NOT Allowed

  • Near intersections
  • On bridges/flyovers
  • Sharp curves
  • Near schools or hospitals
  • At zebra crossings
  • Pedestrian zones
  • Railway crossings
  • Heavy traffic
  • When front vehicle is turning
  • Narrow or single-lane roads
  • Double yellow line areas
  • When police prohibit overtaking
  • At speed breakers
  • Behind large vehicles blocking view

4. Overtaking on Highways

Allowed only when:

  • Left lane = slow vehicles
  • Right lane = overtaking only
  • Use mirrors
  • Flash high-beam briefly to alert
  • Complete overtake quickly

Illegal:

  • Overtaking from left
  • Zig-zag overtaking
  • Sudden lane cutting
  • Following trucks too closely

5. Special Rules for Two-Wheelers

Two-wheeler riders should avoid:

  • Squeezing between cars
  • Overtaking from left
  • Passing trucks in blind spots
  • Speeding during overtake
  • Sudden lane changes

Bikes vanish from mirrors easily → extremely risky.

6. Wrong-Side Overtaking

Overtaking from the left side is illegal because:

  • Drivers don't expect you
  • Left blind spot is bigger
  • Side collisions happen easily
  • Pedestrians usually walk on left

Always overtake from the right only.

7. Fines for Wrong Overtaking

  • ₹500 – ₹1,500 for wrong overtaking
  • ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 for dangerous overtaking
  • Extra penalties if an accident occurs
  • CCTV challans in many cities

8. Safe Overtaking Checklist

Before overtaking, ask yourself:

  • Is the road clear?
  • Is the front vehicle slow enough?
  • Can I return safely?
  • Is there any curve ahead?
  • Could a pedestrian appear suddenly?
  • Do road markings allow overtaking?

If ANY answer is “No”, do NOT overtake.

9. Quick Summary

  • Overtake from right side only
  • No overtaking on curves, crossings, speed breakers
  • Wrong overtaking fine: ₹500–₹1,500
  • Bikes must avoid squeezing between vehicles
  • Overtake quickly and return safely
