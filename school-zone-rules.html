School Zone Rules in India

Speed limits, safety rules, and fines to keep children safe on the road.

1. What Is a School Zone?

A school zone includes:

  • Area near school gates
  • Roads within ~100 meters of a school
  • “School Ahead” boards
  • Zig-zag yellow markings
  • Slow-down signs

These areas require extremely careful driving.

2. Speed Limit in School Zones

Typical limits across India:

  • 20–30 km/h speed limit
  • Slower during school start/end times
  • Speed cameras are common

Driving fast near schools is illegal & dangerous.

3. Mandatory Driving Rules

  • Drive very slowly
  • Keep foot near brake
  • Watch for running children
  • No overtaking
  • No honking
  • Full stop at crosswalks
  • Follow school guards
  • Keep distance from cycles
  • Use low-beam headlights

4. What Drivers MUST NOT Do

  • Overspeed
  • Honk
  • Overtake school buses
  • Stop on zebra crossing
  • Block school gate
  • Use phone while driving
  • Cut between school vans

These actions put children at risk.

5. Rules About School Buses

  • Stop when school bus stops
  • Give priority to children getting off/on
  • Maintain safe distance
  • Never overtake a stopped school bus

Children may run unpredictably.

6. Zig-Zag Yellow Markings

They mean:

  • Slow down
  • No overtaking
  • High caution zone

7. Pedestrian Rules in School Zones

  • Stop at zebra crossings
  • Let children cross first
  • Follow instructions of school staff
  • No aggressive accelerating

Many crossings have CCTV.

8. Fines for Violations

  • ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 for overspeeding
  • ₹500 – ₹1,500 for honking
  • ₹1,000+ for blocking school gate
  • ₹2,000 – ₹5,000 for dangerous driving

Strict enforcement for child safety.

9. Safe Driving Tips for Parents

  • Use only designated drop zones
  • Don’t stop in middle of road
  • Use hazard lights before stopping
  • Check surroundings before moving
  • Teach kids safe crossing habits

10. Quick Summary

  • Speed limit: 20–30 km/h
  • No honking
  • No overtaking
  • Stop fully at zebra crossings
  • Watch for sudden child movement
  • Fines: ₹1,000–₹5,000
