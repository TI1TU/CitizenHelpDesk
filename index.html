FIR vs Complaint vs NC ‚Äì What‚Äôs the Difference?

Many people get confused between FIR, NC, and a Complaint. Here‚Äôs a simple explanation in everyday language.

‚úî FIR (First Information Report)

  • Filed for serious or cognizable offences (the police can arrest without warrant).
  • Examples: theft, assault, major accidents.
  • FIR must be recorded by police when a valid complaint is made.

‚úî NC (Non-Cognizable Complaint)

  • For minor issues like small fights, verbal arguments, minor damage.
  • Police cannot take action without court order.

‚úî Normal Complaint

  • A written application explaining the issue.
  • Police may choose to convert it into FIR/NC based on seriousness.

‚úî When to File What

  • FIR: Serious crime.
  • NC: Minor dispute.
  • Complaint: When you just want to inform police.

‚úî Online FIR

Many states allow filing FIR online for:

  • Lost documents
  • Lost wallet/phone
  • Missing items

Police & FIR Help

Know your rights and how to interact with the police without fear.

üìù How to File an FIR?

FIR (First Information Report) is the first step in a criminal case.

  1. Go to the nearest police station.
  2. Narrate the incident to the officer.
  3. They will write it down. Read it before signing.
  4. Important: You have the right to get a free copy of the FIR.

Note: For minor thefts or lost items, you can often file an "e-FIR" online.

‚öñÔ∏è Your Rights

  • Right to Know: Police must tell you why you are being arrested.
  • Right to Family: You can inform a friend or family member about your arrest.
  • 24-Hour Rule: Police cannot hold you for more than 24 hours without presenting you before a magistrate.
  • Women's Rights: Women cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise (except in exceptional cases with a lady officer).

  • Age limits: 16 for gearless, 18 for full licence.
  • Penalty for driving without licence.

Helmet Rules

  • Rider and pillion must wear helmets.
  • Only ISI-marked helmets allowed.
  • Helmet strap must be locked.
  • Fine for no helmet.

Seat Belt Rules

  • Front seat belt mandatory.
  • Rear seat belt mandatory.
  • Penalty for no seat belt.

Mobile Phone

  • No calling or texting while driving.
  • Hands-free allowed in some states.
  • Penalty for mobile use while driving.

Speed Limits

  • Different limits for city, highways, expressways.
  • Overspeeding fines apply.
  • Speed camera challans are automatic.

Drunk Driving

  • Legal alcohol limit must not be crossed.
  • Breathalyzer test required.
  • Penalty and licence suspension possible.
  • Jail term possible in severe cases.

Number Plate

  • Only standard fonts allowed.
  • No fancy number plates.
  • HSRP plates mandatory.
  • Wrong number plate fine.

Red Light & Stop

  • Stop before stop line.
  • Red light jumping fine.
  • Yellow light rules apply.
  • Cameras generate challans automatically.

Wrong Parking

  • No-parking zones are restricted.
  • Illegal parking can be towed.
  • Challan and towing charges apply.
  • Find towed vehicles at nearest yard.

Lane Discipline

  • Maintain lane while driving.
  • Wrong-side driving is illegal.
  • Lane cutting is punishable.
  • Follow zipper merge rule.

Overtaking

  • Overtake only from right side.
  • No overtaking near turns or bridges.
  • No overtaking at pedestrian crossings.
  • Penalty for dangerous overtaking.

Triple Riding

  • Only 2 persons allowed on two-wheeler.
  • Triple riding fine applies.

PUC Rules

  • Valid PUC certificate required.
  • Penalty for expired PUC.
  • PUC available at authorized centres.
  • DigiLocker PUC accepted.

Insurance

  • Third-party insurance mandatory.
  • Expired insurance penalty applies.
  • Documents can be stored in DigiLocker.

Documents

  • Must carry RC, Licence, Insurance, PUC.
  • All documents accepted in DigiLocker or mParivahan.
  • Challan receipts must be stored safely.

Honking

  • No unnecessary honking.
  • No pressure horns.
  • Silent zones restrict horn use.

  • Auto-rickshaw overloading punishable.
  • Truck weight violations fined.
  • Unauthorized passenger carrying banned.

Lights & Indicators

  • Use indicators for turns.
  • High-beam restrictions apply.
  • Brake lights must work properly.

Pedestrian Crossing

  • Must stop at zebra crossing.
  • No blocking of crossing lines.
  • Pedestrian zones have priority.

Riding on Footpath

  • Riding bike on footpath illegal.
  • Penalty applies.

Children Safety

  • Child seat required for small children.
  • Helmet and harness rules for kids.

Emergency Vehicles

  • Must give way to ambulance.
  • Must give way to fire brigade and police vehicles.
  • Blocking emergency vehicles is punishable.

Traffic Signals

  • Stop, Ready, Go rules.
  • U-turn rules based on signs.
  • No-entry signs must be obeyed.
  • One-way streets must be followed.

Toll & FASTag

  • FASTag mandatory on highways.
  • Double toll without FASTag.
  • Exemptions list available on NHAI guidelines.

  • Check via mParivahan.
  • Check via state traffic police website.
  • Check via e-Challan portal.

Pay Challan

  • Pay using UPI, Card, Netbanking.
  • Official websites only.
  • Download receipt after payment.

Police Stops

  • Police can ask for RC, DL, Insurance, PUC.
  • Documents accepted via DigiLocker.
  • Police cannot take bike keys.
  • No forced bribe allowed.

Accident Rules

  • Inform police immediately.
  • Do not leave the scene.
  • Take photos and videos.
  • Insurance claim procedure required.
  • FIR may be needed.

Police Rights

  • Cannot seize keys without reason.
  • Cannot hit or threaten.
  • Cannot take phone without legal order.
  • Cannot force any payment.
