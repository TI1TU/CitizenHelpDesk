Drunk Driving Rules in India (2025)

Legal limits, penalties, breath tests & safety explained clearly.

1. What Is Considered Drunk Driving?

You are considered drunk driving if you drive after drinking alcohol, or your blood alcohol level crosses the legal limit, or your driving becomes unsafe.

2. Legal Alcohol Limit

Legal limit in India: 30 mg alcohol per 100 ml blood (0.03% BAC).

3. How Police Conduct Checks

  • Breathalyzer test
  • Checking smell & speech
  • Behavior observation
  • Roadside checking points

You cannot refuse a breath test.

4. Fines & Penalties

  • First offence: ₹2,000–₹10,000 fine + up to 6 months jail
  • Repeat offence: ₹3,000–₹15,000 fine + up to 2 years jail
  • Licence may be seized
  • Vehicle may be detained

5. What Happens After Being Caught?

  1. Stopped by police
  2. Breath test performed
  3. Challan issued if above limit
  4. DL may be seized
  5. Court appearance required in some cities

6. Rules for Drinking Passengers

Passengers drinking is not illegal, but drinking inside the vehicle is banned in many states.

7. Rules for Two-Wheelers

Same penalties as cars, but danger is much higher. Police may not allow you to continue riding.

8. Drug-Influenced Driving

Driving under drugs or drowsy medicines is also punishable.

9. Myths vs Reality

  • Myth: “Beer is mild.” → Wrong
  • Myth: “I drive slowly.” → Still unsafe
  • Myth: “Mint will hide it.” → No effect
  • Myth: “Police can't take my licence.” → They can

10. Safe Alternatives

  • Use Ola/Uber/Auto
  • Ask sober friend to drive
  • Use designated driver
  • Stay until sober
  • Use public transport

Quick Summary

  • Legal limit: 30 mg/100 ml
  • Fines: ₹2,000–₹15,000
  • Jail for serious/repeat offences
  • Breath test mandatory
  • Licence may be seized
