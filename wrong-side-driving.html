Wrong-Side Driving Rules (2025)

Wrong-side driving is extremely dangerous and one of the strictest traffic violations in India.

1. What Is Wrong-Side Driving?

Wrong-side driving means driving a vehicle against the flow of traffic or using the incorrect lane to save time.

  • Entering one-way roads in the wrong direction
  • Driving on the wrong lane of divided roads
  • Entering from highway exit sides
  • Cutting across roads to avoid U-turns
  • Riding through gaps near signals

2. Why People Do Wrong-Side Driving

  • Trying to save 1–2 minutes
  • Avoiding proper U-turns
  • Following others doing the same
  • Laziness or lack of awareness
  • “Shortcut” mentality

3. Penalties

  • ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 fine
  • E-challan from CCTV or patrol units
  • Licence suspension for repeat violations
  • Court appearance in serious cases

4. Why Wrong-Side Driving Is Extremely Dangerous

  • High chance of head-on collisions
  • Hitting pedestrians at crossings
  • Chain accidents from sudden braking
  • Vehicles turning legally cannot see you

5. Where It Commonly Happens

  • Near U-turns & medians
  • Market and residential areas
  • Highways (entry/exit ramps)
  • Busy intersections
  • Flyover entry and exit points

6. How Police Catch Wrong-Side Drivers

  • CCTV & ANPR cameras
  • Highway police & patrol bikes
  • Camera vans
  • Manual checking at junctions

7. Safe Driving Rules

  • Always follow lane arrows & markings
  • Take proper U-turns
  • Never cross medians
  • Slow near intersections
  • Do not follow others who drive wrong-side

8. Myths vs Reality

Myth: “It’s just a short distance.”
Reality: Most fatal crashes happen near home.

Myth: “Others are doing it.”
Reality: Their mistake will not save you from challan.

Myth: “Slow speed is safe.”
Reality: Slow head-on collisions are also deadly.

Quick Summary

  • Going against traffic = violation
  • Fines: ₹1,000–₹5,000
  • CCTV-based challans are common
  • Always take proper U-turns
